01, Overview

About Kuwait

Location

Kuwait is a sovereign state in the northeastern corner of the Arabian Peninsula, sitting at the very tip of the Persian Gulf. It is bordered by Iraq to the north and northwest and by Saudi Arabia to the south. Its coastline stretches approximately 499 km along the warm Gulf waters, giving the country a rich maritime identity that shaped its history and economy for centuries.

Culture

Kuwaiti culture is deeply rooted in Islamic traditions and Bedouin desert heritage. Hospitality is not merely a courtesy, it is a sacred obligation. Guests are always welcomed with gahwa (Arabic coffee) and Medjool dates. The country proudly celebrates poetry, falconry, and traditional dhow boat sailing as living national treasures passed from generation to generation.

Language

The official language is Arabic, specifically the Kuwaiti dialect, a distinctive variant of Gulf Arabic enriched with maritime and Bedouin vocabulary. English is widely spoken throughout business, education, and the expatriate community. Kuwait boasts a remarkable 96% literacy rate, one of the highest in the entire Arab world, reflecting the country's long-standing investment in education.

04, Food & Culture

Food & Lifestyle

Click on any card to learn more. Kuwaiti cuisine is bold, fragrant, and deeply communal

In Kuwait, food is never just sustenance. It is an act of hospitality and a declaration of identity. Meals are lavish, shared affairs featuring slow-cooked meats, aromatic rice dishes perfumed with saffron and rose water, freshly baked breads, and rich Gulf seafood. Daily life revolves around dates, the diwaniya (a traditional social hall where community and culture thrive), and the sacred rhythm of prayer times that gently structure every Kuwaiti day.
